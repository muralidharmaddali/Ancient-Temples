Ancient Temples in India



Kailasa Temple

The Kailasa Temple, located in Ellora, Maharashtra, India, is a remarkable rock-cut temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. It's part of the Ellora Caves, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and dates back to the 8th century during the reign of the Rashtrakuta dynasty.

Adi Kumbeshwara Temple

Believed to have been constructed in the 7th century A.D by the Cholas, Adi Kumbeshwara Temple is one of the grandest and the oldest Shiva temple in the town. The temple has a magnificent architecture with the trademark style of the Cholas.

Shore Temple

Built during the 7th century, Shore Temple is one of the oldest South Indian temples constructed in the Dravidian style and depicts the royal taste of the Pallava dynasty. It is located in Mahabalipuram and is one of the most photographed monuments in India situated on the shores of Bay of Bengal.

Dwarakadhish Temple

Dwarkadhish Temple also known as the Jagat Mandir, is a Chalukya styled architecture, dedicated to Lord Krishna. The town of Dwarka has its history dated back to the Kingdom of Dwaraka in Mahabharata. The five storied main shrine is grand and marvelous in itself constructed of limestone and sand. The 2200-year-old architecture, is believed to be built by Vajranabha, who constructed it over the land reclaimed from the sea by Lord Krishna.

Cava Temple

The Cave Temples of Badami consist of four separate temples, each holding intricate carvings and depictions. These carvings depict Shiva and Vishnu in various avatars in the first 3 temples, representing the Brahmanical style. The fourth temple is dedicated to Jain Tirthankaras.

Sun Temple

Built in the form of a giant rath or chariot of the Sun God, it depicts the chariot being pulled by a set of seven horses, four on the left side and three on the right. It has three deities dedicated to the Sun God on three different sides of the temple which catch the direct rays of sun in the morning, afternoon and evening. There is also a dedicated archaeological museum inside the temple complex.

Halasuru Someshwara Temple

Located in the suburbs of Halasuru (Ulsoor) in Bangalore, Halasuru Someshwara Temple is dedicated to the Hindu deity Shiva. Dating back to the Chola period, the temple is the oldest in all of Bangalore. Built in around the 12th and the 13th centuries by the Hoysalas, the temple is now managed and maintained by the Endowment Department of the Karnataka government.

Airavatesvara Temple

Airavatesvara Temple was built by the Chola King Rajaraja Chola II in the 12th century CE. Presided by the Hindu God Lord Shiva, the temple is amongst the eighteen medieval era Hindu temples in the Kumbakonam area. The shrine displays the Vaishnavism and Shaktism legs of Hinduism, and the traditional Nayanars- the Bhakti saints of Shaivism.

Brahadeshwara Temple

The biggest attraction of Thanjavur - The Brahadeshwara Temple, also known as the Big Temple. Built during the reign of the Great Chola emperor, Raja Raja Chola, this temple is an architectural marvel. Protected by deep trenches on either sides and the Grand Anaicut River running through another, this temple is a favorite among the pilgrims. The shrine itself is a 216 feet structure. The sanctum is dotted with paintings from the Chola and Nayak periods. The statue of Nandi the Bull welcomes at the entrance.

Lad Khan Temple

One of the temples that have stood here for the longest of times, the Lad Khan Temple is one of the oldest in Aihole. The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is named after a Muslim Prince who resided here for a while. With the arts and sciences of constructing temples, not as evolved during the time this temple was built, as they were later, the Chalukyas built the temple in a Panchayat Hall style.

Lingaraj Temple

The Lingaraj Temple is an ancient temple situated in the city of Bhubaneswar and is the largest one situated in the city. Dedicated to Lord Shiva as the name suggests, the temple was built in the 7th century by King Jajati Keshari. It is highly revered because of the fact that the Shivalingam, a manifestation of Lord Shiva, is believed to have appeared naturally. It rises to a massive height of 8 inches above the floor level and is about 8 feet in diameter as well.

Sri Venkateswara Temple

The Tirupati Temple, also known as the Tirumala Venkateswara Temple, is one of the most famous Hindu pilgrimage sites located in Tirupati, Andhra Pradesh, India. It is dedicated to Lord Venkateswara, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu, and is a significant center of worship in Hinduism.

Bhogamandeeshwara Temple

Constructed around 806 A.D. by Rathnavalli of Bana Dynasty in the Dravidian style, this temple is known for its ancient Architecture. This temple is located in the Nandi Village and is a good place for the history and architecture enthusiasts. Other such temples in the area are the Yoganandishwara temple and the Sri. Arunachaleswara temple

Badrinath Temple

The Badrinath Temple is one of the most revered Hindu pilgrimage sites, located in the town of Badrinath in Uttarakhand, India. It is dedicated to Lord Vishnu, who is worshipped here as Badrinarayan. The temple is part of the Char Dham Yatra, which includes Yamunotri, Gangotri, and Kedarnath.

Sri Jaganath Temple

The Jagannath Temple, located in Puri, Odisha, India, is one of the most significant Hindu temples dedicated to Lord Jagannath, a form of Lord Vishnu. It is renowned for its annual Ratha Yatra (chariot festival), which attracts millions of devotees and tourists.

Kashi Vishwanath Temple

The Kashi Vishwanath Temple, located in Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh, India, is one of the most sacred Hindu temples dedicated to Lord Shiva, revered as Vishwanath, or "Lord of the Universe." The temple is a key pilgrimage site and holds immense significance in Hindu spirituality.

Ambarnath Temple

The Ambarnath Temple, also known as the Ambarnath Shiv Temple, is an ancient Hindu temple located in Ambarnath, Maharashtra, India. It is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is a significant pilgrimage site for devotees.

Sri RanganathaSwamy Temple

The Ranganathaswamy Temple, located in Srirangam near Tiruchirappalli in Tamil Nadu, India, is one of the most important and largest Vaishnavite temples dedicated to Lord Ranganatha, a form of Lord Vishnu. It is a significant pilgrimage site and is known for its grand architecture and rich history.

Brahma Temple

The Brahma Temple, located in Pushkar, Rajasthan, India, is one of the few temples dedicated to Lord Brahma, the Hindu god of creation. It is a significant pilgrimage site and holds great religious importance in Hinduism.

Kedarnath Temple

The Kedarnath Temple, located in Kedarnath, Uttarakhand, India, is one of the most revered Hindu pilgrimage sites dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is part of the Char Dham Yatra, which includes Badrinath, Yamunotri, and Gangotri.

Meenakshi Temple

The Meenakshi Temple, located in Madurai, Tamil Nadu, is one of the most famous Hindu temples in India, dedicated to Goddess Meenakshi, an incarnation of Parvati, and her consort, Lord Sundareswarar (Shiva). The temple is renowned for its stunning architecture, rich history, and vibrant cultural significance.

Kamakshi Amman Temple

The Kamakshi Amman Temple, located in Kanchipuram, Tamil Nadu, is a revered Hindu temple dedicated to Goddess Kamakshi, a form of Goddess Parvati. It is one of the Shakti Peethas, where the goddess is worshipped as the divine feminine energy.

Chamundeshwari Temple

The Chamundeshwari Temple, located on Chamundi Hill near Mysore in Karnataka, India, is a revered Hindu temple dedicated to Goddess Chamundeshwari, a fierce form of Goddess Durga. It holds significant religious and cultural importance in the region.

Someshwara Temple

The Someshwara Temple, located in multiple places in India, with notable ones in Kolar, Karnataka, and Mangalore, is a significant Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, known as Someshwara or "Lord of the Moon." Each temple has its unique features and historical importance.

Mundeshwari Temple

Mundeshwari Temple, located in the Kaimur district of Bihar, India, is one of the oldest surviving temples in the country. Dedicated to Goddess Mundeshwari, a form of Goddess Durga, the temple is significant both architecturally and historically.

Nandeeshwara Temple

The Nandeeshwara Temple, located in the town of Nandi, Karnataka, is a significant Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, worshipped here as Nandeeshwara. It is known for its rich history, architectural beauty, and spiritual significance.
